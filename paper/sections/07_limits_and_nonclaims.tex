This section centralizes the non-claims; the rest of the paper does not repeat them.

\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=1.5em]
\item No first-principles derivation of the curvature-to-channel map; it is a
  phenomenological ansatz.
\item No claim that the curvature inputs are physically derived: the Riemann/Ricci norms
  are non-derived toy proxies (hand-set constants or Ricci copies, flagged per background
  by \texttt{riemann\_is\_toy}), so $\alpha\lVert\mathrm{Riemann}\rVert_F$ is dimensionless
  and carries no physical units.
\item No external physical validation of the model.
\item No claim that the free calibration parameter $\alpha$ is physically derived; it is
  a demonstrative mid-range setting, and quantitative magnitudes are
  calibration-dependent.
\item No claim that the TTM-inspired proxy is a full transfer-tensor reconstruction.
\item No governance-heavy audit interpretation in this paper; gate and verdict labels are
  implementation-defined classifications (serialized as \texttt{policy\_reason\_code}), and
  the epistemic-governance machinery is out of scope for QSOT2.
\end{itemize}

A passing check therefore means model-internal mathematical consistency under the stated
assumptions --- nothing more. Centralizing these non-claims here is a deliberate
stylistic correction: in the predecessor line they had spread until they dominated every
section.
